<p><p>The digital microscope monochrome camera market primarily features two sensor types: CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device). CMOS cameras are known for their low power consumption, high-speed operation, and integration capabilities, making them suitable for a range of applications. CCD cameras, on the other hand, offer superior image quality with low noise levels and better sensitivity, which is advantageous in high-resolution imaging. Both types cater to diverse fields, including biology, materials science, and semiconductor inspection.</p></p>
