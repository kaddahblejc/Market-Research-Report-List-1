<p><p>The medical cyclotron market is divided into low energy and high energy types based on their energy output. Low energy medical cyclotrons typically operate below 20 MeV and are primarily used for producing radioisotopes for diagnostic imaging, such as PET scans. High energy medical cyclotrons, on the other hand, operate above 20 MeV, enabling them to produce a broader range of isotopes, including those for therapeutic applications. Both types play critical roles in enhancing nuclear medicine and advancing personalized healthcare.</p></p>
