<p><p>Fuel cell technology encompasses several types, each with unique characteristics and applications. Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC) operate at high temperatures, ideal for large-scale power generation. Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC) are known for their quick startup and efficiency, making them suitable for transportation and portable power. Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC) are versatile, used in stationary applications due to their high efficiency. Direct Methanol Fuel Cells (DMFC) utilize methanol directly for power, and Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFC) offer reliable performance for industrial applications. Together, they drive advancements in clean energy.</p></p>
