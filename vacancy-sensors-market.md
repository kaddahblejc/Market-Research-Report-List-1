<p><p>The vacancy sensors market comprises various technologies designed to detect occupancy levels in spaces. Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors detect body heat and motion, making them effective for lower-cost systems. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to identify movement, offering greater coverage and sensitivity. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine PIR and ultrasonic methods to enhance accuracy and reduce false activations. Other types may include microwave sensors and light-level sensors, catering to diverse applications in energy management and automated lighting control in commercial and residential settings.</p></p>
