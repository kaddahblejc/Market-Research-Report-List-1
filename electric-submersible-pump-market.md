<p><ul><li>Low Temperature ESP</li><li>High Temperature ESP</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electric Submersible Pumps (ESPs) are categorized primarily into Low Temperature ESPs and High Temperature ESPs based on the operational temperature of the fluids they handle. Low Temperature ESPs are designed for environments typically below 150°F, suitable for conventional oil and water extraction. In contrast, High Temperature ESPs are engineered to operate in extreme conditions, exceeding 300°F, making them essential for deep wells or geothermal applications. Each type is optimized for efficiency, reliability, and durability under specific temperature conditions.</p></p>
