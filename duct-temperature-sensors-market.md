<p><p>Duct temperature sensors are critical in HVAC systems for monitoring and controlling air temperature within ductwork. Resistance sensors, like PT100 and PT1000, measure temperature by detecting changes in electrical resistance with temperature fluctuations, offering high accuracy and stability. Thermocouple sensors, on the other hand, utilize two different metal wires joined at one end, generating a voltage proportional to temperature differences. Both sensor types play vital roles in optimizing energy efficiency and maintaining indoor comfort in residential and commercial applications.</p></p>
