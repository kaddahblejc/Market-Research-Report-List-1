<p><ul><li>Contact Type</li><li>Non-Contact Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Optical biometry devices are categorized into two primary types: contact and non-contact. Contact type devices require direct tissue contact for measurements, offering precise data on ocular parameters. They are typically used in settings like cataract surgery. Non-contact type devices, on the other hand, measure ocular dimensions without physical contact, enhancing patient comfort and reducing infection risk. These devices are often utilized in routine eye exams and research, making them versatile tools in both clinical and research environments.</p></p>
