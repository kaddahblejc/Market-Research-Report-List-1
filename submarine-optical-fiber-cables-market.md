<p><p>The submarine optical fiber cables market comprises two primary types: deep-sea fiber optic cables and shallow sea optic cables. Deep-sea fiber optic cables are designed for deployment in greater ocean depths, typically exceeding 1,000 meters, providing high-capacity data transmission over long distances. In contrast, shallow sea optic cables are used in coastal and less deep areas, often for shorter connections between land and nearby islands or offshore facilities. Both types are essential for global telecommunications infrastructure.</p></p>
